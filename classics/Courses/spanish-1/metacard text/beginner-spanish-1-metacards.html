Pinyin Tones

Chinese is a language that uses different tone inflections to distinguish between similar characters. Every character has a tone and there are a total of five distinct tones. When the characters are represented in Pinyin, which is the standard way to depict the character phonetically, the tone is written with line(s) on top of the word.

ā á ǎ à

Pinyin Tones

First tone: ā

The first tone is level. It is represented with a straight horizontal line.

  • bā

    八 eight

  • mā

    妈 mother

  • yī

    一 one

  • wān

    弯 to bend

  • chū

    出 out

  • yōu

    优 excellent

Pinyin Tones

Second tone: á

The second tone rises in pitch. It is represented with an upward diagonal line.

  • bá

    拔 to pull

  • má

    麻 hemp

  • yí

    宜 should

  • wán

    玩 to play

  • chú

    除 except

  • yóu

    由 from

Pinyin Tones

Third tone: ǎ

The third tone falls and rises. It is represented with a line that dips down and then up.

  • bǎ

    把 to put

  • mǎ

    马 horse

  • yǐ

    以 to

  • wǎn

    晚 night

  • chǔ

    楚 clear

  • yǒu

    有 to have

Pinyin Tones

Fourth tone: à

The fourth tone falls in pitch. It is represented with a downward diagonal line.

  • bà

    爸 father

  • mà

    骂 to curse

  • yì

    易 easy

  • wàn

    万 ten thousand

  • chù

    处 place

  • yòu

    右 right

Pinyin Tones

Fifth tone: a

The fifth tone is a neutral-pitch tone. It is represented with no line.

  • ba

    吧

  • ma

    吗

  • de

    的

  • me

    么

  • le

    了

  • ne

    呢

Pinyin Alphabet

Now that you learned about the five different tones in Chinese, the next step is to combine the tones with the actual pronunciation of the characters. Certain consonant and vowel sounds are different than they are pronounced in English. For example, the 'q' sound is said with a 'ch' sound rather than a 'k' sound in Chinese.

Character Radicals

Even though you can type Chinese on a keyboard using Pinyin, writing Chinese is a different story. A Chinese radical is a graphical component of a Chinese character. Together, the radicals make up the written structure of the character, and often give it semantic or phonetic meaning. The shape and position of these radicals are also important.

亻 扌 也 ⽑ ⼏

Character Radicals

Radicals 1

  • .....tā

    亻 + 也 = 他

    亻 person

  • .....tā

    女 + 也 = 她

    女 female

  • .....hǎo

    女 + 子 = 好

    子 child/son

  • .....wǎn

    日 + 免 = 晚

    日 sun

  • .....ān

    宀 + 女 = 安

    宀 roof

  • .....nán

    田 + 力 = 男

    力 strength

Character Radicals

Radicals 2

  • .....bà

    尣 + 巴 = 爸

    尣 father, 巴 bā

  • .....mā

    女 + 马 = 妈

    女 female, 马 mǎ

  • .....gē

    可 + 可 = 哥

    可 kě

  • .....nǐ

    亻 + 尔 = 你

    亻 person, 尔 you

  • .....mēn

    亻 + 门 = 们

    亻 person, 门 mén

  • .....jiā

    宀 + 豕 = 家

    宀 roof, 豕 hog
